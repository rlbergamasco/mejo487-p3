World Energy Consumption Over Time

By Riley Bergamasco

Published October 31, 2021

Have you ever wondered how much energy you're using? How about your country? As technology advances, energy use changes along with it. Primary energy consumption is the measure of the total energy demand of a country. This measure includes oil, coal, nuclear, wind, solar, and more.

The map below shows the total primary energy consumption measured in terawatt-hours (TWh) and the primary energy consuption per capita measured in megawatt-hours (MWh) of each country from 1965 to 2016. One terrawat hour is equivilent to one million megawatt hours.

Overall primary energy consumption increased drastically worldwide from 43,248 TWh in 1965 to 152,945 TWh in 2016, a 254 percent increase. However, once you take into account the growing population of the world, the increase is not quite as drastic. The worldwide primary energy consumption per capita only increased 58 percent from 12.95 MWh in 1965 to 20.49 MWh in 2016.
